Departments and Branches

We all know how engineering is divided into different branches depending upon the field of study and its application. Frosh brings you an insight of the branches and departments under which they are categorised in the Institute to clear out any confusion or distraught. Here’s a rundown of the various branches offered at Thapar Institute of Engineering and Technology and a brief description of what it has to offer.

computer

CSED

Computer Science and Engineering Department

The Computer Science and Engineering Department Focuses on the latest technological trends in the world of computers and develops programming skills required on zero ground by incorporating advanced up to date teaching facilities with the old school learning curriculum.

ECED

Electronics and Communication Engineering Department

Electronics and Communication Department is preparing a new generation of Engineers to meet the challenges of future by providing them with a firm foundation by its quality technical education, innovation and state of the art research facilities.

EIED

Electrical and Instrumentation Engineering Department

With its top-notch teaching and research facilities, the department offers BE in Electrical Engineering, Electronics (Instrumentation and control) Engineering and Biomedical Instrumentation as well as postgraduate.

MED

Mechanical Engineering Department

With highly experienced faculty, and laboratories with latest industrial and research technologies, the Mechanical Engineering Department is a pivotal part of the institute's foundation.

CED

Civil Engineering Department

The Civil Engineering Department of Thapar Institute of Engineering and Technology, which has a history as old as the Institute itself, lays emphasis on preparing the students to conceptualize, plan, design and construct the facilities meant for the development of our society’s present and the future.

CHEM

Chemical Engineering Department

Functioning for over 3 decades, this department amalgamates dynamic trends in Chemical Engineering with mathematics and natural science to expand the horizon of possibilities in research and development.

BTD

Department of Biotechnology

Biotechnology is an emerging multidisciplinary branch which primarily focuses on the application of biological and chemical process in the different fields of engineering.

Grading System

A wise man once said- "Grades don’t define an individual, but a student’s CGPA is important as it aids them in their upcoming college life and future plans". While one may not feel the pressure to focus on maintaining a high GPA as they did in high school, there’s no reason why they shouldn’t work hard in their college classes and succeed academically. So, all the instructions related to the grading system of Thapar Institute of Engineering and Technology are mentioned in this section.
Each academic semester includes:

  • MST(Mid Semester Tests)
  • Sessionals
  • EST(End Semester Tests)

CGPA is the weighted average of all the grades awarded to a student since his/her entry into the Institute.

A+, A, A-, B, B-, C & C- grades: These grades are the pass grades.
A+: This grade is awarded in rare cases. Even the student with maximum marks doesn’t get this grade, in order to get this grade, the performance of the student should be exceptional and the class strength should be atleast 100.
E, F, I, X grades: If these grades are awarded in any course then that course shall be termed as backlog course.
E: This grade is awarded in the case when the attendance is minimum 75% but fails in the evaluation process
F: This is awarded when a student has very low score even after appearing in ESTs even though his/her attendance is greater than 75%. In this case the student has to register again for the course.
I: This is awarded when the student has good academic record but is unable appear in the ESTs.
X: This is awarded due to the shortage of attendance. This student has to register again for the course.


Important Points
  • In auxiliary examination the students with E grade will be awarded maximum of C- grade.
  • A student will be allowed to continue a course at the end of
    • First Year, if the student:
      • Secures a CGPA >= 3.70
      • 2. Earns a minimum of 50% of the credits offered in the approved scheme of courses in the first year
    • Second Year, if the student:
      • Secures a CGPA >= 4.50
      • 2. Earns a minimum of 60% of the credits offered in the approved scheme of courses in the first and second year
  • Minimum attendance required to avoid X grade is 75%

“Students should not let one bad grade define their abilities. It’s a minor setback. How they choose to overcome it, is what sets them apart from the rest of the world.”
